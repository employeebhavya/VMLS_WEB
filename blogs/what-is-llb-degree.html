What is the LLB Degree ? : Significance, Eligibility, Major Subjects, and Careers

By VMLS

Jan 27, 2025

0 Views

What is the Significance of an LLB Degree?

LLB stands for Bachelor of Legislative Law, which includes understanding several legal aspects. This course is the first step to becoming a lawyer or solicitor and solving legal matters. It is a 3-year programme, however, in some cases, it can extend up to 5 years, if opted with specialisation. The law course consists of several subjects like Contract law, Civil law, Corporate law, Criminal law, and many more. This course is very significant as it prepares students by providing them with knowledge about legal principles and skills necessary to start a career in corporate law, legal journalism, civil services, and many more domains. So, let’s discuss some key aspects related to LLB in detail, for more clarity regarding the programme.

What is the Eligibility Criteria For an LLB Degree?

The LLB eligibility depends not only on a proper academic background but also on some major analytical, communication, and research skills. Some of the most important skills required to pursue a law degree include:

  • Analytical thinking
  • Decision making
  • Negotiation skills
  • Time management
  • Strong communication
  • Research and analysis
  • Ability to work in a team
  • Attention to detail
  • In-depth understanding of legal concepts and principles

Along with all these skills, it is very essential to have one’s necessary education completed. To be eligible for admission in an LLB course in India, students need to complete their 10+2 education with a minimum percentage in any stream. However, some colleges require their students to have studied English, History, or Political Science in their 12th Grade as a major subject.

Your 10+2 qualification is essential to get admission to a good law college. You also need to take relevant entrance examinations to get admission to the top universities. Some of the major LLB entrance examinations in India are:

  • CLAT (Common Law Admission Test)
  • LSAT (Law School Admission Test)
  • AILET (All India Law Entrance Test)
  • DU LLB Entrance Exam (Delhi University LLB Entrance Exam)
  • MH CET Law (Maharashtra Common Entrance Test for Law)

These are some of the major exams that one can opt for to get admission to a top law college. However, there are several other examinations too. The eligibility criteria depend on several factors, and students must prepare accordingly based on their college requirements. Also, the course fees vary based on the type of university and the provided facilities.

What are the Different Types of LLB Degrees?

After LLB eligibility, the next step is to choose the type of degree one wishes to pursue. Some programmes can be completed in three years, while some take five years to complete. The three-year LLB programme is the traditional route to completing one’s degree with a specialization in law. However, the five-year integrated programmes combine undergraduate programmes in other domains along with the LLB degree.

Some major integrated programmes are BA LLB, BBA LLB, and BCom LLB. These courses include law with arts and humanities, business administration and management, and commerce, respectively. The traditional three-year course is offered in many countries after completion of a bachelor’s degree in any discipline like arts, science, commerce, etc. Whereas, the five-year course includes a bachelor’s degree in another domain and an LLB programme. The first three years are focused on undergraduate courses (BA, BBA, BCom, etc.), and the remaining two years focus on law studies.

So, if you have already completed the bachelor’s degree then the three-year programme is best suited to you. On the other hand, the integrated programme is suited for those who haven’t completed their bachelor’s degree. Also, it needs a proper commitment of five long years.

What are the Major Subjects included in the LLB Degree?

There are many compulsory and elective subjects to be studied while pursuing an LLB. This usually depends on the course offerings of the Law University you choose. The first two years are mostly dedicated to compulsory subjects, and the third year consists of elective subjects. However, you must study all the subjects properly.

Some of the major compulsory subjects are as follows:

  • Contract law
  • Criminal law
  • Constitutional law
  • Property law
  • Tort law
  • Legal research and writing
  • Public interest law

Whereas, the elective subjects offered by universities could include the following:

  • Administrative law
  • Tax law
  • Company law
  • Labour law
  • Environmental law
  • Family law

Studying all these subjects at a good and well-established Law university can help you understand the in-depth knowledge provided in the course, thus helping you become a good lawyer or legal counsel.

What are the Career Opportunities after completing LLB?

Once you complete your law degree, you are prepared to work in various job profiles. There is a wide range of career opportunities available in this domain. Some of the major highly paying opportunities are as follows:

  • Lawyer
  • Law officer
  • Judge
  • Corporate counsel
  • Legal consultant
  • Legal advisor
  • Public prosecutor
  • Solicitor
  • Law professor

These are a few professions one can pursue after completing the law course. However, based on your interests, you can also consider opting for other career paths or for higher education.

Conclusion

So, this was the essential information one must have before taking the first step towards an LLB degree. It is an important undergraduate degree that provides the skills and knowledge necessary to start a career in law. One must make sure to do due diligence properly and then prepare for the future ahead.
