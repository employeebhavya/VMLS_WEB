3-Year LLB Programme at VMLS: Eligibility, Admission Criteria, Subjects, and Career Scope

By VMLS

Jan 27, 2025

0 Views

The 3-year LLB (Bachelor of Legislative Law) programme is an undergraduate programme designed to cater for students who don’t wish to pursue five-year integrated law courses like BBA LLB, B. Com LLB, or BA LLB. The three-year LLB programme is a qualifying degree to practice law legally. Hence, completing the LLB programme can enable you to work in government offices, NGOs, judiciary, corporate offices, and many more.

So, to cater to several students wishing to build their careers as a lawyer, Vinayaka Mission’s Law School (VMLS) has introduced the three-year LLB programme. VMLS is the best college to pursue LLB, as it offers the best amenities, teaching facilities, and amazing job opportunities after completing the degree. To know more about the eligibility criteria, admission process, and subjects associated with this course, you can give this blog a quick read.

Eligibility Criteria for Three Year Law Programme

To be eligible for the three-year law programme at VMLS, you need to complete your bachelor's degree in any stream from a university or an equivalent institution recognized as deemed to be a university or from a foreign university declared as equivalent to an Indian degree by a competent authority. Candidates who are about to proceed for their final degree exams can also apply, however, they must have their degrees with them during the admission process.

Also, to be eligible for admission, you must have not less than 45% marks for General category students. For OBC, the minimum required marks are 42%, and for SC/ST candidates it is 40%.

Admission Criteria for the LLB Programme

The admission criteria are quite simple to get admitted to the three-year LLB programme at VMLS. The university needs your VLAT (VMRF Law Aptitude Test) Score and your 10+2 or equivalent marks. If you are eligible for admission then you can get a seat in VMLS.

The following steps will enable you to know more about the admission process at VMLS:

  1. Register yourself for VLAT, and verify the email address.
  2. Fill in the online application form.
  3. Pay the required application fees.
  4. Apply with all the other required documents.
  5. The application will be processed internally.
  6. Appear for the VLAT Exam.
  7. After the results, you’ll get your admission offer letter through mail.
  8. Make sure to complete seat blocking and fee payment.
  9. Lastly, you’ll get your provisional admission letter.

All these steps will enable you to get admission into VMLS without causing any chaos. And, you’ll be able to study at the best law school in Tamil Nadu. If you face any difficulties while completing the admission process, then you can contact the team at VMLS and they will resolve all your doubts quickly.

Compulsory Subjects for the Three-Year Law Programme

While completing your LLB degree from VMLS, you’ll be required to complete a few common subjects before commencing with the core law subjects. However, it is essential to study these subjects as it will help you to lay a strong foundation for your career in law, and help you to learn the core law subjects with ease. The subjects are as follows:

  1. Political Science
  2. Legal and Constitutional History
  3. Foundations of Research
  4. Sociology
  5. Organizational Behaviour
  6. Numerical Thinking
  7. Economics

After completing these subjects in your initial semester, you’ll then be able to study the core law subjects. It will include subjects like Family law, Taxation law, Corporate Law, Contract Law, Criminal Law, and a few other laws which you can either choose as elective subjects or as main subjects. It will enable you to get a clear perspective of the field and help you to decide your plan further. Faculties at VMLS explain subjects in the best way, to help you learn better and think clearly about concepts.

Career Scope After Completing the LLB Programme

There are plenty of job options available for you after completing your three-year LLB programme. If you wish to practice as a lawyer then you need to clear the AIBE (All India Bar Exam) conducted by the BCI. However, there are some other job profiles in the government and non-government organizations for LLB degree holders. So, you can work as a legal consultant, judicial officer, public prosecutor, compliance officer, Human Rights lawyer, legal researcher, law professor, etc.

Also, if you wish to study further, then you can complete your LLM and PhD degree in any law subject of your choice, and then grow in your career. Also, top recruiters like AZB & Partners, S&R Associates, Trilegal, VakilSearch, and many more offer amazing job opportunities for LLB degree holders.

What Skills Will Students Gain After Completing the Three-Year LLB Programme?

After completing the 3-year LLB programme from VMLS, you’ll not only gain the essential skills but also get a strong network with industry experts. Upon successful completion, the graduates will possess a comprehensive skill set encompassing effective written and verbal communication, critical thinking, logical reasoning, analytical thinking, and efficient problem-solving abilities.

They will have acquired a robust foundation across all studied courses, demonstrating proficiency in conducting high-quality legal research and the preparation, examination, evaluation, and execution of legal documents. Furthermore, graduates will exhibit a profound understanding of contemporary social issues, engaging with them in a creative and solution-oriented manner. This holistic approach extends to instilling a sense of social responsibility, encouraging active participation in civic duties, and fostering a commitment to ongoing self-reflection and lifelong learning.

So, studying at VMLS will enable law graduates to solve several real-life problems, and make things easier for the common public, institutions, and various private and government organizations.

So, LLB graduates can work as legal advisors for enterprises, become magistrates, pursue legal journalism, become human rights activists, or research for non-government or government institutions. Hence, there is a huge career scope after completing the 3-year LLB programme. Also, if you wish to pursue a master’s degree after completing the LLB then you can opt for the LLM degree programme as well. It will enable you to work as a professor or researcher in the legal domain.

However, you must complete your degree from one of the top law colleges in India. So, VMLS stands out as a great university for this purpose providing all the essential facilities. Also, it enables you to connect with industry experts, get expert insights, and learn from the best faculties.
