Learn About the Core Subjects in Law Courses: A Precise Guide for Legal Professionals

By VMLS

Jan 27, 2025

0 Views

If you are willing to work in a legal advisory firm, judiciary, or as a lawyer, then pursuing a law degree plays an important role in your career. However, there are several subjects law students are supposed to study. It may include constitutional law, taxation law, family law, and so on. Also, most of these subjects are common in a three-year and five-year law degree.

The only difference is that a five-year law degree allows you to study other disciplines like business, arts, commerce, science and technology. The five-year degree has courses namely the BA LLB, B.Com LLB, B.Tech LLB, B.Sc LLB, and BBA LLB. And, the three-year degree course i.e. LLB can be completed after your graduation which solely focuses on law subjects. However, to dive deep into the law subjects you can opt for several specialization courses or diplomas, after your post-graduation.

Major Law Subjects for Law Aspirants in Indian Law Colleges:

Before pursuing your LLB degree, having little knowledge about law subjects is essential. There are several law subjects, but major subjects include constitutional law, property law, etc. While other subjects can be chosen as electives along with the major subjects. To know more about the law subjects, you can check out the following details:

  1. Constitutional Law:

    As the name suggests, constitutional law includes the study of different principles and procedures involved in government activities. It includes knowing about the rules and regulations that need to be followed while running a nation. It is the best subject for law students interested in legislative, executive, and judiciary practices. This subject not only helps students know about the workings of government bodies, their relationship with the country's people, and other government bodies for easy functioning.

  2. Criminal Law:

    Lawyers practising criminal law are involved in resolving criminal offence occurrences, trial processes, fixing offence charges, and various related procedures. They are generally aligned towards protecting society and maintaining peace in daily life. Criminal laws differ in different countries and must be studied according to the nation’s policies. In India, any college’s law course details can provide necessary details regarding the topics included while studying criminal law and other related subjects.

  3. Civil Law:

    Civil law involves regulating the obligations and rights of individuals, along with the legal relations between them. It is associated with non-criminal matters like property, divorce, money, contracts, etc. Law colleges in India focusing on this subject may involve various topics like contract law, family law, and torts law for a better understanding. One must understand that civil law is different from criminal law and common law. Hence, it is essential to study this subject while pursuing your LLB degree.

  4. Property Law:

    The study of property law allows you to know the rules and regulations related to resources like land or personal property. These rules are different as per the different types of property, and many law colleges in India teach this subject due to its greater importance. It involves regulating the transfer of property either movable or immovable from one individual to another. While pursuing an LLB, property law is taught as a major subject, and law students get to know various laws and rules associated with it.

  5. Environmental Law:

    The rise in global environmental conditions resulted in the establishment of strict laws for environment conservation in each country. As a result environmental law plays a major role, and it stresses the importance of climate change, disposal laws, effluent disposal, environmental protection, harmful waste management, etc. Law students with a master’s degree in environmental law can build a strong career in this field. They can work as environmentalists, lawyers, socialists, and in several professions.

  6. Corporate Law:

    It is essential to maintain governance while managing businesses, so, corporate law plays a major role in this regard. It includes the corporate jurisdictions, management, and other topics related to companies and their working. Some common cases under corporate laws include share issues and frauds, management issues, investor issues, and many more. An LLB degree with a specialization in corporate law can land you a great job in companies as a corporate lawyer or consultant with a great salary.

  7. Human Rights Law:

    Human Rights laws are important in today’s world, and lawyers practising them ensure the right to livelihood, political rights, social rights, and several rights on the grounds of humanity. It is usually offered as an elective subject along with the other compulsory BA LLB subjects. However, majoring in this subject can allow you to work with great organizations like the United Nations. Several top law colleges in India provide various specialization courses and degrees focusing on human rights law.

  8. Intellectual Property Law:

    The intellectual property law ensures the protection of inventions and discoveries by a person or company from unfair practices. It allows you to study the intricacies of copyrights, trademarks, licenses, patents, and research. This law helps individuals to protect their rights for their inventions and allows them to legally publish or use their inventions. To know more about this subject, you can check out the law course details provided by the law college, it will help you get in-depth knowledge about it.

    These were a few of the main law subjects that must be studied by law aspirants to get deeper insights into law. Some other law subjects are contracts, cyber law, maritime law, transnational law, energy and land law, taxation law, labour law, and many more. Based on your interests, you can either choose these subjects as electives along with your major degree, or you can also pursue a diploma or specialization course in any of these subjects.

Conclusion

That was all the necessary details regarding the various law subjects you need to study while pursuing your LLB. Make sure to choose the right college while pursuing your degree, as it helps you to learn things from the top professors, with the right guidance.
