Why Do You Need to Pursue LLM in Commercial Dispute Resolution?

Commercial Dispute Resolution is in practice for decades and mechanisms such as arbitration and negotiation are of much need as per the law. The rise of more effective, decisive, and cost-effective resolution of disputes makes LLM in Commercial Dispute Resolution a great program for staying relevant in the industry. This use of this mechanism is seen increasing not only in domestic but also in international businesses. Hence, law professionals with knowledge of subjects like commercial mediation, international and domestic arbitration, enforcements of settlements, and investor-state dispute resolution are obtaining better career opportunities.

As a result, an LLB degree course in Commercial Dispute Resolution is the best choice for law graduates. It provides a holistic understanding of international and domestic dispute resolution processes. Also, it not only helps you gain theoretical knowledge but also helps you understand the policies of dispute resolution, and many more terminologies, thus expanding your knowledge as a lawyer.

Eligibility Criteria to Pursue LLM in Commercial Dispute Resolution at VMLS

To be able to pursue LLM in Commercial Dispute Resolution at VMLS, you must match the eligibility criteria. Firstly, you need to have a bachelor in law (LLB) from a university or an equivalent institution recognized as deemed to be a university or from a foreign university declared as equivalent to an Indian degree by a competent authority. Satisfying all these mandatory requirements makes you eligible for this course.

Also, candidates who are about to proceed with their final exams can apply, however, they must have their degrees with them during the admission process. If you’re eligible as per the requirements then you’ll be able to complete your LLM specialization in Commercial Dispute Resolution from the law university in Chennai and make your resume much stronger and better.

Admission Process for LLM in Commercial Dispute Resolution at VMLS

To get admitted into the LLM program in Commercial Dispute Resolution at VMLS, you need to follow a few easy steps. The university needs your VLAT (VMRF Law Aptitude Test) Score, 10+2 or equivalent marks, and a bachelor in law (LLB) from any university. If you are eligible for admission then you can get a seat in VMLS in their highly valued LLM specialization.

Steps for LLM program in Commercial Dispute Resolution Admission:

  1. Register yourself for VLAT, and verify the email address.
  2. Fill in the online application form.
  3. Pay the required application fees.
  4. Apply with all the other required documents.
  5. The application will be processed internally.
  6. Appear for the VLAT Exam.
  7. After the results, you’ll get your admission offer letter through mail.
  8. Make sure to complete seat blocking and fee payment.
  9. Lastly, you’ll get your provisional admission letter.

All these steps will enable you to get admission to the LLM program in Commercial Dispute Resolution at VMLS without any issues if all your documents are correct. And, you’ll be able to study at the best colleges for law in Tamil Nadu.

What are the Various Subjects in this Program?

The LLM in Commercial Dispute Resolution degree has two semesters, and you need to pass all the subjects to complete your degree properly. The total weightage of these subjects is twenty-six credits, and each semester has six different subjects.

Semester 1:

Course Name Credits
Research Methods and Legal Writing 3 Credits
Law and Justice in a Globalized World 3 Credits
Comparative Public Law and Governance 3 Credits
International Commercial Arbitration 2 Credits
Commercial Courts: Practice and Procedure 2 Credits
Dissertation Writing Course - I 1 Credits

Semester 2:

Course Name Credits
Corporate Insolvency 2 Credits
Optional Course - I 1 Credits
Optional Course - II 2 Credits
Capstone Project and Internship 3 Credits
Dissertation 3 Credits
Dissertation Writing Course - II 1 Credits
