Explore the Diverse Career Opportunities in Law in India: Roles, Necessary Skills, and Top Employers

Make sure to get admission to the best law college , develop the right skills, and explore various career opportunities that interest you. Best Career Opportunities in Law in India

Once you complete your law education, various career options will be available to you to shape your future. A law career is challenging but rewarding, and having the best skills helps law students to pursue their dreams. There are many law-related job opportunities in India. The following are a few most common job roles that can help you grow rapidly in this field:

  • Advocate:

    Law students often look forward to starting their careers as advocates. After completing the LLB degree, students choosing this path can practice in the District Court, High Court, and the Central Supreme Court. However, students need to clear the All India Bar Council examination to qualify to start their practice as an advocate. Initially, students join as junior assistants to senior advocates, where the task is to research cases and draft papers. With practice, they can work as independent lawyers.

  • Corporate Lawyer:

    Corporate law firms provide many opportunities to freshly graduated law students. After completing your advocate course, students can target the best law firms to start their careers. It is considered one of the best law fields in India, where lawyers counsel big companies either as full-time counselors or as employees. The task of a corporate lawyer is to ensure that the organization complies with all legal requirements and policies, to avoid any legal issues that may disturb the company’s reputation.

  • Legal Advisor:

    After completing your law study, starting a career as a legal advisor is a great venture. Legal advisors provide advice related to legal issues to individuals, companies, and the corporate sector. Their main tasks include drafting papers and resolving disputes. To practice as a legal advisor, a state license and passing the bar exam is required. A law career as a legal advisor is challenging in the beginning, but with practice, one can earn good money from it.

  • Judge or Magistrate:

    To play a major part in administrative affairs, one can pursue a career as a judge or magistrate. Graduates must hold an LLB degree from a recognized university to be eligible for such posts, spend a significant amount of time practicing law, and be under the age of 35. The High Court supervision or Public Service Commission fills the posts of Magistrates or Sub-Judges. Based on seniority, one can get promoted to the High Court or the Central Supreme Court at different posts.

  • Government Services:

    Students interested in government services can work in the Department of Legal Affairs as legislative counsel or legal advisors. Law graduates can also work in Indian Legal Services, Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force to work on legal issues. The tasks performed by these professionals include conducting hearings, drafting papers, and managing legal matters. Law professionals working in such government services get good salaries, pensions, and allowances for their families.

  • Law Professor:

    Many bright students with strong academics and a master’s or Ph.D. degree in law pursue careers as law professors. Top universities or law schools in India require such graduates, and this has a huge scope. Candidates can also work as visiting professors in private colleges, helping students gain insights from the best. Many top colleges pay great salaries to professors/lecturers with immense knowledge about the advocate course who can shape the students' bright futures.

  • Legal Researcher and Journalist:

    Law journalists and legal researchers contribute immensely to the media industry. They contribute to print media like magazines and newspapers, along with electronic media like news channels and websites. Law graduates who love studying current affairs and are updated with the latest legal news can start their career in this field. They play a major role in educating people about the latest legal matters, such as corporate investments, updates in the economy, and fund transfers to various sectors.

    So, these are the major job roles that can help law graduates kick-start their careers after their law study is completed. Some other job roles that are attracting law students are criminologist, investigator, paralegal, solicitor, and many more.

Necessary Skills for Law Graduates:

To work in any of the above-mentioned job roles, one must have the necessary skills to develop a career in law. Having skills like effective communication, research, analytical thinking, problem-solving, writing, and attention to detail are most important. Just having a good academic background is not enough due to the cut-throat competition. Having a good skill set helps immensely, and employers hire candidates with such skills. Therefore, students must develop analytical, communication, and problem-solving skills to work in top law firms or government services right after graduation.
