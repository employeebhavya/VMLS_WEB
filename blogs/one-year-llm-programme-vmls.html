What is the Significance of the LLM Programme at VMLS?

The LLM Programme at VMLS offers a unique blend of academic rigour, innovative teaching methods, and industry exposure. All these features enable VMLS to shape future leaders in law. The LLM programme at VMLS is significant as it focuses on collaborative group projects, hands-on capstone experiences, interactive discussions, expert-taught lessons, and real-world legal and corporate challenges that enable students to tackle problems.

Hence, the programme ensures both practical and academic excellence, along with industry-relevant experience.

Eligibility Criteria for the One-Year LLM Programme

To be eligible for the one-year LLM law programme at VMLS, you need to have a bachelor's degree in law (LLB) from a university or an equivalent institution recognized as deemed to be a university or from a foreign university declared as equivalent to an Indian degree by a competent authority. Candidates who are about to proceed for their final exams can also apply, however, they must have their degrees with them during the admission process. If you’re eligible as per the requirements then you’ll be able to study from the best law college in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. VMLS offers an LLM programme in corporate and financial laws and commercial dispute resolution.

Admission Process for the One-Year LLM Programme

The admission process for the one-year LLM programme is quite straightforward to get admitted at the VMLS. The university needs your VLAT (VMRF Law Aptitude Test) Score, 10+2 or equivalent marks, and a bachelor's degree in law (LLB) from any university. If you are eligible for admission then you can get a seat in VMLS in their highly valued LLM programme.

The following steps will enable you to know more about the admission process at VMLS:

  1. Register yourself for VLAT, and verify the email address.
  2. Fill in the online application form.
  3. Pay the required application fees.
  4. Apply with all the other required documents.
  5. The application will be processed internally.
  6. Appear for the VLAT Exam.
  7. After the results, you’ll get your admission offer letter through mail.
  8. Make sure to complete seat blocking and fee payment.
  9. Lastly, you’ll get your provisional admission letter.
