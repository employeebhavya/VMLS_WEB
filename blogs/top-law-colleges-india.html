Law Degrees at the Best Law Schools in India

When admitted to a law college in India, you can pursue a five-year or three-year degree course. The five-year degree contains law as the major subject along with other subjects. Some of the common degrees are B.Com LLB, BA LLB, BSc LLB, etc. On the other hand, the three-year LLB degree focuses completely on various law subjects like corporate law, constitutional law, family law, and many more. Later, if you wish to complete your post-graduation after completing the law degree you can opt for the LLM degree too.

Hence, choosing the best law colleges in India is very essential to complete your law degree with the right facilities. The top colleges like NLUs (National Law Universities) and other top private law colleges allow students to build a strong career in law and grow in their respective fields.

    • National Law Institute University (NLIU) Bhopal

  2. Tier 2 NLUs:

    • Chanakya National Law University (CNLU) Patna, Bihar
    • Rajiv Gandhi National University of Law (RGNUL) Patiala, Punjab
    • Ram Manohar Lohiya National Law University (RMLNLU) Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh
    • National University of Advanced Legal Studies (NUALS) Kochi, Kerala

  3. Tier 3 NLUs:

    • Tamil Nadu National Law University (TNNLU) Trichy, Tamil Nadu

Eligibility Criteria for the Best Law Colleges in India

You must satisfy a few necessary criteria to get admission to the top law schools in India. You must get good marks in law entrance exams like the CLAT, AILET, VLAT, etc. Also, you must understand the prerequisites properly before applying to any law college. To apply to any law university in India, you must have completed your diploma or your 12th-grade examination with a minimum of 45%. This cut-off is lowered to 40% for students from the SC and ST communities. And, entrance exams are required for students who wish to apply to a particular university. A good score in competitive exams is a must to get admitted to the top law colleges.

Law School Entrance Exams in India:

There are various entrance exams based on the universities, states, etc. All these exams help students to choose the best college for their further education. Some of the common entrance exams are CLAT (Common Law Admission Test), AILET (All India Law Entrance Test), VLAT (VMRF Law Aptitude Test), and many more. The scores of CLAT are considered by more than a hundred law schools in India. Also, it is the most common exam for law aspirants in India.

Whereas, the AILET is conducted by NLU Delhi and is focused on that one university. The VLAT is conducted by the VMLS Law College and is focused only on admissions in that college. Also, VMLS provides scholarships for eligible students to support them in their academic and career goals. Hence, it stands out as the best private law college in India.
