Know About the Various Types of Law Courses in India: A Complete Guide for Aspiring Lawyers

By VMLS

Jan 27, 2025

0 Views

Many students in India choose law as a career due to the various benefits this field offers. Although there are several challenges associated with pursuing a legal career, with proper education, you can have a great life in this domain. If you are interested in skills like critical thinking, analytical thinking, and problem-solving then pursuing an LLB degree in India will be a great choice. There are several three-year and five-year degree programs available, and you can choose the program as per your field of interest and specialization. Consider giving this blog a quick read, explore the various courses, their benefits and duration, and choose the LLB degree course that best suits you.

What is the Difference Between 5-Year and 3-Year Degree Courses?

Before choosing the right course for your education, it’s necessary to consider the law course duration. So, in India, you can complete your LLB in either 5 years or 3 years. The 5-year course is a pretty popular choice for students as it combines law studies with a specific discipline. It can include combining law with subjects like arts, business, science, commerce, or technology. The 5-year degree can be opted for right after completing your 12th-grade studies. On the other hand, a 3-year degree is chosen after completing a bachelor's degree. It offers exposure to various law subjects like constitutional law, criminal law, and many more. It helps students to seek opportunities across diverse fields.

Different Law Degrees at the Undergraduate Level

After completing your 12th grade, there are several 5-year integrated law programs that you can opt for. These courses help students to get knowledge about both legal and general subjects. Here are some of the most popular courses that you can choose:

  1. BA LLB:

    The BA LLB course combines arts and legal subjects and helps students kickstart their law careers. It includes subjects like economics, history, political science, sociology etc. Also, it enables students to learn law-related subjects like criminal law, contract law, and constitutional law. This course provides the best understanding of how law works in society and its usage in research, public service, etc.

  2. BBA LLB

    Students with an interest in business administration and the legal subjects associated with it can choose a BBA LLB degree. This LLB course provides exposure to management principles, corporate governance, and business law. Students wishing to work in a corporate environment can choose this LLB degree course and start their career by contributing to the corporate world.

  3. B.Sc LLB:

    B.Sc LLB is another integrated course that allows students to learn law with science subjects. It can include subjects like physics, chemistry, and biology, along with legal subjects. Students having an interest in environmental law or intellectual property law can choose this law course. It also provides a skill set that can be used in industries like pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and many more.

  4. B.Com LLB:

    If you wish to learn subjects like taxation law, labour law, or corporate law then B.Com LLB is the best degree for your career. It focuses on commercial laws required in businesses and prepares individuals to manage legal issues related to financial services, trades, etc. After completing this course, individuals can even work in financial institutions, or corporations, or start their own ventures.

  5. B.Tech LLB:

    The B.Tech LLB degree emphasizes engineering along with the legal subject. Students wishing to pursue engineering and then get a career in law can opt for this course. Many best law colleges in India provide this course, and it contains legal subjects along with technical subjects like electronics, electricals, computer science, etc. It can lead students to start a career as a technology consultant.

    These were the five most preferred integrated law courses that can be completed in five years directly after completing the 12th board exams. There are many other courses like B.SW LLB and B. Pharm LLB, and one can choose them as per the choice.

Various Law Degrees at the Post Graduate Level

After completing the graduation students can excel in their field by completing their post-graduation and reaching the depths of the subjects. For this purpose, several three-year law programs can help students in their careers. The following are the most essential law courses in this regard:

  1. LLB:

    The LLB degree course is a three-year program that enables students to explore various legal subjects like property law, criminal law, family law, environmental law, and many others. Students wishing to practice law as a full-time career can choose this law course and reap the benefits. The significance of an LLB degree in India is growing extensively, and serving as an advocate is still one of the best career options.

  2. LLM:

    The LLM (Master of Laws) is a specialized degree that provides exposure to certain areas of legal subjects like corporate law, environmental law, and international law. It allows law graduates to dive deep into their field and gain more knowledge about the subject in one or two years. Hence, it improves the specialization of lawyers in a particular field and enables them to grab better opportunities.

  3. PG Diploma in Law:

    A diploma degree in law is best for students who have completed their education in another field and wish to study law. It provides specialization courses in various legal subjects like corporate law, taxation law, and cyber law. Pursuing a diploma course can make your resume stronger and open more opportunities in your career. It is typically a shorter duration course ranging from 6-12 months.

  4. PhD in Law:

    The highest academic qualification in the legal field is to get a PhD in Law. Having a PhD degree from the best law colleges in India can shape your career drastically. It is majorly focused on research and is best suited for students aspiring to careers in the research industry, academic careers, or consultancy roles. It also allows students to specialize in certain law subjects as per their interests.

    These were the four key law programs one can consider at the post-graduate level. However, certain other programs like the M.Phil in law, MBA LLB, LLD, and MCL can benefit you in your career as a lawyer. Also, if you are unable to pursue any of the above courses because of the law course duration, then you can opt for the specialization courses. These specializations help you learn subjects like corporate law, criminal law, family law, and many other subjects in six months or one year.
