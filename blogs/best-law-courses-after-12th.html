Best Law Courses After 12th: Types of Courses, Eligibility, Duration, and Career Opportunities

By VMLS

Jan 27, 2025

0 Views

Many students are developing their interest in law and several fields related to legal studies. After the 12th exam, students are confused about the course to pursue and the career ahead. So, having information regarding the best law courses in one’s interests and getting admission to the best law colleges in India is very important. So, get the various law course details by reading this article.

Types of Law Courses After 12th:

After the 12th, one can consider taking a 5-year law course, a diploma course, or certificate courses in law. As per the course duration, syllabus, fees, and your career plan you can choose the course. The five-year courses are integrated programs like the BA LLB, B.Com LLB, B.Sc LLB, BBA LLB, and B.Tech LLB. On the other hand, the diploma and certificate courses are of variable duration. The following details will help you to know about the integrated courses in-depth:

  1. BA LLB:

    The most common law course is the BA LLB (Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Legislative Law). This course contains subjects related to humanities like sociology, history, political science, and economics. Along with law-related subjects like CPC, corporate law, labour law, criminal law, and several other subjects. The course is best to understand social issues and develop critical and analytical thinking.

  2. B.Com LLB:

    B.Com LLB (Bachelor of Commerce and Bachelor of Legislative Law) is another 5-year law course that combines commerce and law subjects. It is best for students who wish to learn business-related subjects like auditing, statistics, economics, and accounting. The course also offers law-related subjects like consumer law, contract law, criminal law, constitutional law, and other related subjects.

  3. B.Sc LLB:

    To pursue law along with science one can consider taking the B.Sc LLB (Bachelor of Science and Bachelor of Legislative Law) course. This course enables interested students to learn scientific subjects like physics, chemistry, biology, etc. Also, it covers legal topics like contract law, legal theory, etc. Having a B.Sc LLB degree will help you solve cases requiring scientific knowledge, too.

  4. BBA LLB:

    BBA LLB (Bachelor of Business Administration and Bachelor of Legislative Law) is based on business-related aspects like commerce, business studies, management principles, computer applications, business communication, quantitative techniques, etc. Along with this, it teaches you law subjects like taxation law, mergers and acquisitions, business law, corporate law, and many more subjects.

  5. B.Tech LLB:

    Pursuing a B.Tech LLB (Bachelor of Technology and Bachelor of Legislative Law) course is unusual, but the benefits of this course are immense. The course not only covers law subjects but also undergraduate-level engineering subjects like operating systems, electronic circuits, embedded systems, and many more. It is usually a 6-year integrated course and contains 12 semesters.

Diploma Courses in Law

After the 12th, instead of choosing a 5-year law course, you can choose a diploma course in law. This helps you to reduce the duration of the course and get better employment opportunities with the acquired skills and knowledge. There are several diploma courses available, but the following are the most-suited diploma courses available after 12th:

  • Diploma course in Cyber law
  • Diploma course in Business law
  • Diploma course in Criminal law
  • Diploma course in Human Rights law
  • Diploma course in Taxation law

Some other diploma courses related to law focus on labour law, criminology, corporate law, information technology law, and many more. As per your interests, you can choose the diploma course and start your career in a particular niche related to law.

Certification Courses in Law

Certification courses are similar to diploma courses in law. They can be completed in less time and are focused on a single subject. Also, the fees required to complete certification courses are lesser compared to the LLB course fees. Following are some of the most common law certification courses:

  • Certification course in corporate law
  • Certification course in business law
  • Certification course in human rights
  • Certification course in cyber law
  • Certification course in consumer protection law

The above-mentioned are the most common law certification courses. However, there are law-related certification courses that focus on other subjects, too.

Eligibility Criteria for Law Courses

The eligibility criteria for various law universities may differ based on their requirements. But, while pursuing law after the 12th few criteria are the same for all the universities. Firstly, the candidates must have scored a minimum of 45% in their 10+2 examination or equivalent examination from a recognized school/college. SC/ST students must have scored at least 40% or equivalent marks. There is no age limit as per the latest guidelines. Also, candidates appearing for the qualifying examination are allowed to apply for law courses. However, during the admission process candidates must provide a certificate of the qualifying examination.

Along with the 10+2 marks, candidates also need a good score in their entrance exams to get admission to the top colleges. The following are some of the most common law-related entrance exams:

  • CLAT (Common Law Admission Test)
  • AILET (All India Law Entrance Test)
  • LSAT (Law School Admission Test)
  • DU LLB (Delhi University LLB Entrance Test)
  • SLAT (Symbiosis Law Admission Test)

There are several state-level tests too, that can help you to get admission to the top law colleges in your respective states. Having a good score in 10+2 and the entrance examination will help you get admission to the top law colleges. Some private universities may also offer discounts on the LLB course fees if your scores are higher.

Duration for Law Courses

The above-mentioned law courses require different durations to complete them. The following details will help you to know the duration of the law courses:

  • LLB – 3 years
  • BA LLB/B.Sc LLB/B.Com LLB/BBA LLB – 5 years
  • B. Tech LLB – 6 years
  • Diploma Courses – 1 year
  • Certificate Courses – 3 months to 2 years
  • LLM – 2 years
  • Post Graduate Diploma Courses – 1 to 2 years

So, these were law course details about some of the most common courses and the time required to complete them. The fees for all the courses vary based on the colleges and their fee structure and it can range from 1 Lakh to 15 Lakhs depending on the course, college, and course duration.

Career Opportunities after Law Courses

A law degree provides students with critical, analytical, problem-solving, and communication skills. This opens the door for them to several opportunities in fields like law, government agencies, human resources, education, and more. Further studies in law can help students to understand complex legal matters, and prepare them for advocacy roles and policymaking. Law graduates can also start their careers by working with firms like Khaitan & Co., Trilegal, AZB & Partners, etc. Hence, students should pursue a law degree properly, contribute to society, and also create better opportunities for themselves.
